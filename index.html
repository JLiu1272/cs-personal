Youtube Randomizer

During my fall semester at RIT, I had to take introduction to music to full-fill my general education credit. The class turned out much harder than expected, particularly for students who are toned-death like me.


During each of the exams, our professor would randomly pick a song at a random time from the 15 songs that he gave us. We would be expected to write down the song we think it is. The problem was most of the songs were classical music, so to me they all sounded the same. My friend and I would often fail this part of this exam, which meant failing half the exam.


To alleviate the situation, we created a simple program with javascript, html, and css. Given user youtube link inputs, our program would play one of links given, and play it at a random time.


Current improvements: - We are going to add a quizing feature

Linkur - No More 404

A site that strives to resolve broken link issue.

In 2016, I participated in my first hackathon at Cornell with a friend. One of the problems we encountered on stackoverflow was that many of the useful links provided my others were often deprecated or domain has changed. This problem became more apparent when learning older technologies such as Objective C, which now is proceeded by the newer swift. Many of the solutions became unhelpful because the links that were provided just did not work, so the answer itself made little sense.


We created this site where you can cache the link, so no matter what happens to the link, it will still be usable. However, the site has been taken down since it was using up our money, and it was really just a small thing we wanted to try out for the hackathon.


Configuration Environment:

- Amazon Web Services
- Laravel

SMIF - Smart Meals, Intelligent Fridge

Teammates: Harshvardhan Gupta, Kyaw Khant Nyar, Robert Aguilera, Christine Van, Candy Wang

1st Prototype has been completed.

SMIF (Smart Meals Intelligent Fridge) aims to make your food management a breeze by keeping track of what you eat in an unobtrusive and intuitive way.It remembers what you put in your fridge and is therefore capable of suggesting recipes, reminding you when you're running low on items, and informing you on food freshness. SMIF helps you have a healthy and balanced diet. It uses IOT devices and Machine Learning to achieve these things.

The project is currently being improved. Throughout our summer break, we hope to add more features. The new features include:
